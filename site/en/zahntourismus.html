Health Insurance Reimbursement

Your German health insurance contributes — clearly regulated by EU law

Since Croatia joined the EU in 2013, EU Directive 2011/24/EU on cross-border healthcare applies. Patients with statutory insurance receive the same fixed subsidy as for treatment in Germany — on the basis of Section 13 (4/5) SGB V (German Social Code, Book V).

How the fixed subsidy works

The statutory health insurance (GKV) reimburses the diagnosis-based fixed subsidy for standard care under Section 55 SGB V. This is a fixed amount determined by your diagnosis — regardless of whether you are treated in Germany or in Zagreb. With a well-kept bonus booklet, your share increases:

Important: dental prosthetics require prior approval

Dental prosthetics and implants must be approved by your health insurance fund before treatment begins. Without an approved treatment and cost plan (HKP) in place before treatment, there is no subsidy. So always submit the HKP in good time — we prepare it for you.

Honestly — what the GKV does not cover

For implants or high-quality ceramics, the statutory fund usually pays only the fixed subsidy for standard care. You bear the additional amount above that and your travel costs yourself. In return, you benefit from a 2-year warranty on dental prosthetics — and we plan aftercare in from the very start. That way you know exactly where you stand in advance.

Please note: This is not legal or tax advice. Please clarify the exact reimbursement individually with your health insurance fund. The basis is EU law on cross-border healthcare (EU Directive 2011/24/EU, Section 13 SGB V).

Frequently Asked Questions

Clearly answered — honest and complete

The most important questions about reimbursement, the process and your care.

Will my health insurance pay for treatment in Zagreb? +
Yes. As a patient with German statutory insurance, EU law (EU Directive 2011/24/EU, Section 13 SGB V) entitles you to the same diagnosis-based fixed subsidy as for standard care in Germany. For dental prosthetics there is one condition: the treatment and cost plan must be approved in advance. You bear the amount above standard care, as well as your travel, yourself.
How does the treatment and cost plan (HKP) work? +
We draw up your HKP on the official German insurer's form. You submit it to your health insurance fund before treatment begins. The fund usually takes around 3 weeks to review and approve the fixed subsidy. Only then does treatment start — that way the subsidy is secured.
Which implants do you use? +
We work with the same premium brand systems as leading German practices: Straumann and Nobel Biocare. These systems are documented worldwide, proven over the long term and well tolerated — the quality is identical, no matter where you are treated.
How many trips should I plan for? +
That depends on your diagnosis. Many treatments can be bundled into just a few appointments. Implants usually require a healing phase, so two visits can make sense. We plan the appointments with you in advance, so that trips and waiting times are kept to a minimum.
What about warranty and aftercare? +
We provide a 2-year warranty on dental prosthetics. We plan aftercare in from the start: we stay reachable, schedule check-ups and, where needed, coordinate with your local dentist. Your long-term dental health is at the centre.
In which language will I be looked after? +
Our team communicates with you in Croatian, English and Italian. Our website and your enquiry can be in German; a German-speaking digital assistant is in preparation. A personal contact person accompanies you throughout.
